## Findings

### 1. A2L / R-454B Refrigerant Transition — Customer Education Gap

- **Relevance: 10 | Novelty: 9**
- Sources: EPA AIM Act, ACCA A2L Training, manufacturer transition guides (Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Daikin, Mitsubishi), ACdirect, SNARSCA, Cooling Company, Surplus City, ifactoryapp, PracticeTestGeeks
- The 2025 manufacturing ban on new R-410A residential split systems is now fully in effect, and 2026 new-equipment sales are essentially A2L only (R-454B dominant for splits, R-32 for many ductless lines). The transition generates a steady stream of customer questions — "is my refrigerant banned?", "why is the quote higher?", "is A2L dangerous?" — that sales and CSR staff are answering inconsistently. No skill in the repo addressed this yet; it is the single highest-impact customer-facing HVAC-specific change of the year.
- Flammability facts (LFL ~9.5% for R-454B vs. 5% natural gas, 2.1% propane), repair-vs-replace math, proper charging (liquid-state only for R-454B blend), and system-matching rules are all anchor points contractors should standardize on.
- The 25C federal heat-pump tax credit angle is tightly related — customers with completed 2025 installs can still claim the credit on 2026 returns, and many are confused about eligibility windows.
- **Action taken:** Created new skill `customer-service/a2l-refrigerant-explainer.md` — produces verbal talking points, emails, one-pagers, SMS, or in-home leave-behinds that anchor to regulatory facts, cover repair-vs-replace framing, and defuse flammability concerns without fear-mongering. Also created `knowledge-base/regulations/a2l-r454b-transition.md` as the factual anchor document all future refrigerant-related skills should reference.

### 2. Speed-to-Lead Response Workflow

- **Relevance: 9 | Novelty: 7**
- Sources: Hatch (HVAC-specific benchmark data), Apten (2026 speed-to-lead benchmarks), ACHR News, Ignitvio, Leadsync, Minyona
- 60-second lead response converts at 391% the rate of responses after 5 minutes, and 88% of HVAC inbound responses currently take longer than 5 minutes. Top-performing campaigns use a 7-touch cadence (5 SMS + 2 email) over 5 days; single-message outreach sits around an 8% response rate. This is a clean prompt-based workflow — the bottleneck is not technology, it's having a consistent script and cadence to load into whatever CRM or SMS platform the contractor uses.
- Existing skills don't address net-new lead handling; `review-responder.md` covers post-job communication, and `invoice-followup-drafter.md` covers collections, but there was no front-end lead-handling skill.
- **Action taken:** Created new skill `sales/speed-to-lead-qualifier.md` — generates the first-touch SMS (under 320 chars, human-feeling), a full 5-day 7-touch cadence across SMS + call + email, a CSR qualifying script with emergency-phrase triage, and urgency-track variations. Includes explicit guardrails against quoting prices over the phone and against generic "Team"-signed messages.

### 3. After-Hours AI Call Handler — Finally Ready to Build

- **Relevance: 9 | Novelty: 6**
- Sources: Dialzara, Upfirst, Smith.ai, Voiceflow, Botafide, Avoca, Podium ("Larry"), ServiceTitan AI Voice Agents, EVS7, newo.ai, NextPhone, Epiphany Dynamics
- Carried from 4/12 and 4/13 backlog. The ecosystem of HVAC-specific AI voice agents has matured enough that emergency-keyword triage ("no heat", "no cooling", "gas smell", "water leak", "CO alarm"), appointment booking, and dispatcher handoff are table stakes. Published pricing tiers (~$49 basic / $149 scheduling / $299 advanced) and consistent feature lists across vendors indicate this is now a standardized problem with a standardized solution shape.
- The missing piece for most contractors is not the vendor — it's a vendor-agnostic script and triage policy document that can be loaded into Podium, ServiceTitan AI, Avoca, or a live overflow service interchangeably.
- **Action taken:** Created new skill `customer-service/after-hours-call-handler.md` — produces the voice-agent opening script, a 3-level emergency triage decision tree (dispatch tonight / book first-thing tomorrow / daytime queue), minimum viable caller-record template, pricing-conversation rules, objection-handling for the three most common pushbacks, out-of-service-area etiquette, and a dispatcher handoff package format. Usable for a voice AI vendor or a human answering service.
